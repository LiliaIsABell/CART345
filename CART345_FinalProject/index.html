You Can Say It

For my final project, I wanted to create an interactive activity that reproduces how it feels to struggle to say certain words. I have met many people who have difficulty saying certain simple sentences such as “I love you”, “I am sorry” or “I disagree”. They often feel awkward saying those words either because of the way they were raised or because they are uncomfortable with being vulnerable.

The words moving around represents the mind running and being overwhelmed by the multitude of thoughts going around. I chose to make the activity drag and drop because I felt that it emphasizes the struggle of finding the right words to say and selecting them.

The purpose of this project is to encourage the participant to say those words and hopefully get to a point where they no longer feel embarrassed to say them.

This project was made using jQuery for the activity and Annyang to allow voice recognition. Because of the use Annyang, this project will be more effective if used on Chrome.

In a blue box, there are words moving around within it. These words can be dragged and dropped to the blank box below to form one of the sentences below:

- I am sorry
- I was wrong
- I love you
- I need help
- Please stop
- I want this
- In my opinion
- I disagree

If one of these are formed, the screen will clear ending the activity and you can stop or refresh the page to try again. However, dragging and dropping is too difficult, you can say it. Say the sentence loud and comprehensively and the screen will clear.
